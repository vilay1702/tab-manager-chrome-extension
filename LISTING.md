## 3. Detailed description (≤ 16,000 characters)

```
Tired of 80 open tabs and no way to find anything? Stash gives you a tidy
side panel that lives next to your browsing — drag tabs into folders, pin
your favorites, and reopen anything you closed by accident.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
WHAT YOU CAN DO
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

📁  ORGANIZE INTO FOLDERS
Create colorful folders for projects, research, reading lists, or
whatever you need. Drag any tab into a folder to save it for later.
Folders collapse so the panel stays clean.

⭐  FAVORITES, ONE CLICK AWAY
Pin up to 8 sites to a favorites row at the top. Press ⌘D / Ctrl+D
to favorite the current tab instantly.

🪄  AUTO-ORGANIZE
One click groups similar open tabs into folders automatically — perfect
for taming a window that has gotten out of hand.

🔍  SEARCH EVERYTHING
Type in the search box (or press /) to filter live tabs, favorites,
and saved tabs at once. Press Esc to clear.

♻️  RECENTLY CLOSED
Reopen any tab you closed in the last 48 hours from a single button.
Up to 100 recent closures kept.

🎯  DRAG AND DROP
Drag tabs between live tabs, favorites, and folders in any direction.
Reorder folders, reorder tabs inside folders, and rearrange favorites
with the same gesture.

🧹  CLOSE ALL
Archive every tab in the window with one click (pinned tabs stay open).
Combine with auto-organize to clear the deck without losing anything.

⌨️  KEYBOARD SHORTCUTS
  •  ⌘N / Ctrl+N — new folder
  •  ⌘D / Ctrl+D — favorite the current tab
  •  /          — focus search
  •  Esc        — clear search

⚙️  SETTINGS
Optional: re-open your saved tabs on browser startup so your project
folders are ready to go each morning.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
WHY A SIDE PANEL?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Most tab managers steal a whole tab or pop up a tiny dialog. Stash uses
Chrome's native side panel, so your saved tabs sit beside whatever
you're reading — no context switch.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
PRIVACY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Stash works entirely on your device. Nothing is sent to a server,
nothing is tracked, nothing is sold. Your tabs and folders stay in
your browser's local storage.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  •  tabs       — to read tab titles/URLs you stash and reopen
  •  storage    — to save your folders and favorites locally
  •  sidePanel  — to show the side panel UI

  •  alarms     — to clean up old "recently closed" entries
```

## 6. Privacy disclosures (Web Store form)

### Single purpose

> Stash is a tab and bookmark manager. It lets users save open tabs into folders, pin favorites, and restore recently closed tabs from a Chrome side panel.

### Permission justifications

Copy these into the corresponding fields on the dashboard.

| Permission | Justification |
|---|---|
| **tabs** | Required to read the title, URL, and favicon of open tabs so users can save them into folders, mark favorites, and search across tabs. Also used to activate, close, and reopen tabs from the side panel. |
| **storage** | Required to persist the user's folders, favorites, settings, and recently-closed list locally in the browser. |
| **sidePanel** | Required to display the extension UI in Chrome's native side panel. |
| **favicon** | Required to display site icons next to saved tabs in the UI. |
| **alarms** | Required to periodically prune entries older than 48 hours from the "recently closed" list. |
| **Host permissions** | None requested. |
| **Remote code** | The extension does not load or execute any remote code. All scripts are bundled. |

## 9. Pre-submission checklist

- [ ] Bump `version` in `package.json` from `0.1.0` to `1.0.0` (or your launch version)
- [ ] Update `manifest.config.ts` `name` to `Stash - Tab & Bookmark Manager`
- [ ] Update `manifest.config.ts` `description` to match the short description above
- [ ] Add `icons` block to `manifest.config.ts` (16, 48, 128) and ship the PNGs in `/public` or `/src/assets`
- [ ] Add `action.default_icon` to `manifest.config.ts`
- [ ] Run `npm run build` and verify `dist/` loads cleanly via "Load unpacked"
- [ ] Test on a fresh profile: install, pin, open side panel, create folder, drag tab in, search, recently closed, auto-organize, close-all
- [ ] Test in light + dark mode
- [ ] Zip the `dist/` folder for upload (`cd dist && zip -r ../stash-v1.0.0.zip .`)
- [ ] Capture screenshots at 1280×800 *(use Chrome DevTools device toolbar at exact resolution)*
